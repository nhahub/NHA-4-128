## General Risk Factors

- High UV exposure significantly increases risk of melanoma, BCC, SCC, and actinic keratosis.
- Fair skin (Fitzpatrick type 1-2) is associated with higher UV sensitivity and cancer risk.
- Previous skin cancer history increases recurrence risk.
- Immunosuppression (e.g., from organ transplants or HIV) elevates malignancy risk.
- High mole count (>50) and atypical moles are melanoma risk factors.
- Sunburn history, especially blistering sunburns in childhood, raises lifetime risk.
- Genetic mutations (CDKN2A, BAP1, BRCA2, MC1R, TP53) are linked to hereditary skin cancer.
