## Key Clinical Notes

- Melanoma is the most dangerous form of skin cancer and requires urgent biopsy.
- Basal cell carcinoma (BCC) is the most common malignant skin cancer, usually slow-growing.
- Squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) can metastasize if untreated.
- Actinic keratosis is a pre-cancerous lesion caused by UV damage.
- Nevus (mole) and benign keratosis are typically non-cancerous but require monitoring.
- Dermatofibroma is a benign skin nodule, rarely malignant.
- Vascular lesions include hemangiomas and other blood vessel growths, mostly benign.
