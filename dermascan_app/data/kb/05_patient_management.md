# Patient Management and Follow-Up Guidelines

## Biopsy Indications

- Any lesion with asymmetry score >0.7 or border irregularity >0.7.
- Lesions >10mm that have changed in color, shape, or size.
- Patients with genetic mutations (CDKN2A, BAP1, TP53) and new lesions.
- Lesions with ulceration, bleeding, or crusting.
- Suspected melanoma or SCC based on clinical appearance.

## Follow-Up Frequency by Risk Level

- **Low risk (hereditary score <0.3, no prior cancer)**: Annual skin check.
- **Medium risk (score 0.3–0.6 or family history)**: Every 6 months.
- **High risk (score >0.6, known mutation, prior cancer)**: Every 3 months.
- **Post-biopsy confirmed malignant**: Every 3 months for first 2 years, then every 6 months.

## Immunosuppressed Patients

- Immunosuppressed patients (organ transplant, HIV, autoimmune medications) are at 65x higher risk of SCC.
- These patients should have dermatology visits every 3 months regardless of risk score.
- Any suspicious lesion in immunosuppressed patients should be biopsied immediately.

## Patient Education

- Apply SPF 50+ sunscreen daily, even on cloudy days.
- Avoid tanning beds — they increase melanoma risk by 75%.
- Perform monthly self-exams using the ABCDE rule.
- Wear protective clothing and seek shade between 10am–4pm.
- Inform family members about hereditary mutations so they can be screened.
